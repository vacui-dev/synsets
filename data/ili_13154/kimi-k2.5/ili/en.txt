<|ILI_13154|>actinomorphic refers to a <|ILI_083370|>type of biological <|ILI_049752|>symmetry where an <|ILI_000035|>organism or <|ILI_058780|>structure can be divided into two or more identical halves by any longitudinal plane that passes through the central axis. This radial symmetry is characteristic of many flowers, such as those of lilies and buttercups, as well as certain marine animals like sea urchins and starfish. The term is commonly used in botany and zoology to describe organisms that have multiple planes of symmetry around a central point, as opposed to bilateral symmetry which only has one plane.
