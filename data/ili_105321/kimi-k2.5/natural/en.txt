A bracket fungus that grows as a parasite on trees, particularly oak and chestnut. It produces large, fleshy fruiting bodies with a distinctive appearance resembling raw meat or liver, featuring a reddish-pink to brownish upper surface and a porous, whitish underside. The flesh exudes a blood-like juice when cut and has a slightly acidic, meaty flavor. Native to Europe and North America, it is considered a desirable edible mushroom when young and tender, though it becomes tough and woody with age. It causes brown rot in its host trees.
