Shock troops are elite soldiers specifically trained and heavily armed to lead a frontal assault against fortified positions. The concept originated in World War I warfare, where specialized units spearheaded attacks to breach enemy defenses and create openings for the main force. These soldiers typically employ aggressive close-combat methods and carry heavier weapons than regular infantry. Though the term emerged from early twentieth century military doctrine, the concept remains relevant in modern warfare, where contemporary shock units often integrate armored vehicles and precision munitions.
