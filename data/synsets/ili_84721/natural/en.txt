Blacksburg is a small university town located in southwestern Virginia, nestled in the Allegheny Mountains of the Appalachian range. The town is best known as the home of Virginia Tech (Virginia Polytechnic Institute and State University), a major public research university whose campus and student population define much of the community's character. Situated west of Roanoke, Blacksburg serves as a hub for education, technology, and outdoor recreation in the mountainous region of the state. The surrounding landscape offers access to hiking, scenic views of the Blue Ridge, and a temperate four-season climate that attracts both students and retirees. As a quintessential American college town, Blacksburg's economy, culture, and daily life revolve largely around the university and its affiliated research enterprises.
