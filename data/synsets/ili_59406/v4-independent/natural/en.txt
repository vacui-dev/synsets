A squab is a type of soft, heavily padded sofa or settee, distinguished by its exceptionally thick cushioning and low-slung profile. The term derives from the historical use of the word "squab" to describe something plump or cushion-like, reflecting the piece's defining characteristic of generous, pillowy padding across the seat, back, and sometimes armrests. Squabs are typically found in domestic living spaces, where they serve as comfortable seating furniture designed for relaxation rather than formal occasions. Unlike standard upholstered sofas, a squab emphasizes deep, yielding comfort over structured support, often featuring tufted or quilted surfaces that contain the soft filling. The term is somewhat archaic in modern usage, having been largely supplanted by "sofa," "couch," or "divan," though it persists in furniture terminology and interior design contexts.
