When two or more things contrast, they exhibit noticeable differences when compared side by side. The verb "contrast" captures the act of revealing disparity—whether in color, character, quality, or behavior—by placing elements together so that their differences become apparent. In visual art, contrasting colors like black and white create striking effects; in rhetoric, contrasting ideas sharpen an argument. As "counterpoint," this concept extends into music, where two independent melodies play simultaneously, each highlighting the other's distinctiveness through harmonic and rhythmic interplay. The hypernym "be different" anchors this sense as a specific mode of differentiation—one made visible through deliberate comparison rather than inherent dissimilarity alone.
