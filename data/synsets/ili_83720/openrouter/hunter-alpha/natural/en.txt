Libreville is the capital and largest city of Gabon, a republic on the west coast of Central Africa. Situated on the northern shore of the Gabon Estuary near the Gulf of Guinea, the city serves as the nation's administrative, commercial, and political center. Founded in 1849 as a French colonial settlement for freed slaves — its name meaning "free town" — Libreville has grown into a modern urban center with a population exceeding 800,000. The city is home to key governmental institutions, including the Presidential Palace and the National Assembly, and functions as Gabon's principal port for timber and petroleum exports. Libreville's economy is driven primarily by trade, shipping, and the processing of natural resources, including manganese and uranium from the surrounding region.
