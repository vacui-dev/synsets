A catapult is a large medieval siege engine designed to hurl heavy projectiles, such as stones, at fortified walls and defensive structures during a siege. As a type of artillery predating gunpowder weapons, the catapult was one of the most powerful offensive tools in ancient and medieval warfare. The most advanced form, the trebuchet, operated by dropping a heavy counterweight to swing a long arm, launching projectiles over great distances with tremendous force. Catapults transformed the nature of castle warfare, rendering previously impregnable fortifications vulnerable to sustained bombardment. The weapon evolved through several variants—including the mangonel, powered by manpower, and the smaller Roman onager—each adapted to different tactical needs on the medieval battlefield.
