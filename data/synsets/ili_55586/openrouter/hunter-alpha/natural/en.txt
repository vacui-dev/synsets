A laxative is a mild cathartic—a type of medication designed to stimulate or facilitate bowel movements and relieve constipation. Unlike stronger purgatives that can cause intense cramping and abdominal discomfort, laxatives work gently by promoting intestinal peristalsis or softening stool to ease the evacuation of feces. They are commonly recommended for temporary relief of mild constipation and are available in various forms, including tablets, liquids, and fiber-based supplements. As a subclass of cathartics within digestive health pharmacology, laxatives occupy an important clinical role, offering a gentler therapeutic alternative to harsh purgative drugs. The choice of laxative depends on the underlying cause of constipation, patient tolerance, and desired onset of action.
