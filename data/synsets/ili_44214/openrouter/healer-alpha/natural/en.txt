Monitor lizards (genus Varanus) are large, carnivorous reptiles belonging to the family Varanidae. Found across tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia, these lizards are characterized by their elongated bodies, powerful limbs, well-developed tails, and keen predatory instincts. The common name "monitor" originates from an old belief that these lizards warn crocodiles of approaching prey, though this notion is largely mythical. The genus encompasses approximately 80 species ranging from the small short-tailed monitor to the Komodo dragon (Varanus komodoensis), the world's largest living lizard, which can exceed three meters in length. Monitor lizards are active hunters that feed on insects, eggs, birds, small mammals, and other reptiles, and they display a relatively high degree of intelligence among saurian reptiles. As members of the diapsid reptiles, they represent one of the most successful and diverse lineages of modern lizards, occupying ecological niches ranging from arboreal predators to semi-aquatic hunters.
