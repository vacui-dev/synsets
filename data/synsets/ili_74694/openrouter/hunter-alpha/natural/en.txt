An arraignment is a formal legal proceeding in which a defendant is brought before a court to hear the charges filed against them and to enter a plea. In common law jurisdictions, the arraignment represents a critical early stage in criminal proceedings, following arrest and formal charging but preceding trial. During this hearing, the presiding judge reads the indictment aloud, informs the accused of their legal rights, and asks for a plea — typically guilty, not guilty, or no contest. The arraignment serves both as a procedural safeguard and as the accused's first opportunity to respond to the prosecution's charges. It is distinct from a summons or subpoena, which compel appearance, and from an indictment itself, which formally states the charges.
