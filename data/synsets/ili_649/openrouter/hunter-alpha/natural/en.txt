Subsequent refers to something that follows in time or order after a given point or event. It describes items, actions, or phenomena that come later within a sequence or chronology. The term is commonly used in both temporal and logical contexts — for example, "subsequent events" indicates occurrences that took place after a reference moment, while "subsequent chapters" refers to sections that appear later in a text. Unlike a strict successor relationship, "subsequent" does not imply direct causation but merely temporal or sequential ordering. It stands in contrast to "preceding," which denotes what came before. The word appears frequently in academic, legal, and technical writing where precise chronological distinction is required.
