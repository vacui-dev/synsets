Macrencephaly is a rare congenital condition characterized by an abnormally enlarged braincase resulting from excessive growth of brain tissue during development. It is classified as a developmental abnormality that often arises from genetic mutations or disruptions during fetal brain formation. Unlike hydrocephalus, in which cerebrospinal fluid accumulation causes head enlargement, macrencephaly involves actual overgrowth of neural tissue itself. The condition is typically diagnosed through neuroimaging techniques that reveal an oversized cranial vault disproportionate to normal growth patterns. Individuals with macrencephaly may experience neurological complications including seizures, developmental delays, and intellectual disability, though the severity varies considerably depending on the underlying cause and extent of brain overgrowth.
