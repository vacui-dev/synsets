A cutaway is a men's formal coat distinguished by its diagonal cut from the waist to the back of the knees, creating a characteristic silhouette in which the front panels are shortened while the back extends into tails. The design originated in nineteenth-century riding coats, where the front was literally cut away to give equestrians greater freedom of movement in the saddle. Over time, the cutaway evolved from practical riding attire into a cornerstone of morning dress, the semi-formal daytime equivalent of evening tails. It is traditionally worn at formal daytime occasions such as weddings, funerals, investitures, and royal ceremonies, paired with a waistcoat and striped trousers to form the complete morning suit ensemble.
