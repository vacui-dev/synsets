Dry, in this adjectival sense, describes a quality of plainness and deliberate restraint from ornamentation or vivid coloration. It characterizes prose, presentations, or expressions that convey information in a direct, unembellished manner—prioritizing factual clarity over stylistic flair. The term often carries a connotation of severity or austerity, suggesting a stark, mechanical quality that avoids any decorative embellishment in favor of bare, unadorned presentation. Dry writing or speaking tends toward an austere tone that, while potentially informative, may lack the vividness or artistic quality that more elaborate expression provides.
