A written or printed symbol is a visual mark or character that has been inscribed, typeset, or otherwise rendered onto a surface such as paper, stone, or screen. These symbols form the fundamental building blocks of all writing systems, ranging from alphabetic characters used in Latin-script languages to logographic symbols employed in Chinese, Japanese kanji, and ancient Egyptian hieroglyphs. Written and printed symbols serve as the primary technology for recording human thought across time, enabling the preservation and transmission of knowledge independent of oral tradition. In modern usage, the concept extends beyond traditional media to encompass digital typography, where the same symbols are rendered through pixels and vector graphics. The distinguishing feature of a written or printed symbol, as opposed to other visual signs such as pictorial images or gestures, is that it operates within a conventionalized system of representation — each symbol carries meaning by agreement among a community of users rather than by inherent resemblance to what it represents.
