Obfuscation is the deliberate act of rendering something unclear, ambiguous, or difficult to understand. It is a technique commonly employed in communication to obscure the true meaning of a message, whether through overly complex language, intentional vagueness, or the strategic omission of key details. In the realm of information technology, obfuscation refers to the practice of making code or data intentionally difficult to interpret, serving as a protective measure against reverse engineering. The concept stands in direct opposition to clarity and transparency, and is often associated with deception, mystification, and the purposeful bewilderment of an audience. While obfuscation may serve legitimate purposes — such as protecting intellectual property or ensuring security — it is frequently viewed as a barrier to honest and effective communication.
