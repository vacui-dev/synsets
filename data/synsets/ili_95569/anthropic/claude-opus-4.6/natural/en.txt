Oliver Wendell Holmes Jr. was an American jurist who served as an Associate Justice of the Supreme Court of the United States from 1902 to 1932. Known for his progressive judicial philosophy and eloquent legal writing, he championed judicial restraint and the principle that law should evolve with society's changing needs. His famous opinions on free speech, including the "clear and present danger" doctrine in Schenck v. United States, established foundational precedents for First Amendment jurisprudence. Holmes is widely regarded as one of the most influential justices in American legal history.
