Reinforcement is a fundamental concept in behavioral psychology referring to any stimulus that follows a behavior and modifies the probability of that behavior occurring again. In operant conditioning, a framework developed extensively by B.F. Skinner, reinforcement serves to increase the likelihood of the preceding response, whereas its absence or the introduction of punishment tends to decrease it. Positive reinforcement involves presenting a rewarding stimulus after a desired behavior, while negative reinforcement involves the removal of an aversive stimulus to achieve the same effect. The concept extends well beyond the laboratory into education, parenting, workplace management, and therapeutic interventions, where systematic application of reinforcement schedules shapes learning and habit formation.
