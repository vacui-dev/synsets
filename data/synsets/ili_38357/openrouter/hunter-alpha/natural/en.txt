The give-and-go is a fundamental offensive maneuver in basketball in which one player passes the ball to a teammate and immediately cuts toward the basket to receive a return pass. This two-player action exploits the brief moment when defenders are focused on the ball handler, creating an opening near the hoop for a high-percentage scoring opportunity such as a layup. The play requires precise timing, spatial awareness, and trust between the two participating players. As one of the simplest yet most effective coordinated movements in team basketball, the give-and-go is taught at all levels of the sport, from youth leagues to professional competition. It is classified as a type of basketball play, a broader category encompassing any preset plan of action executed by a team during offensive possession.
