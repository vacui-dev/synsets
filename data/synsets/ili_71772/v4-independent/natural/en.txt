A brickbat is a harsh, disparaging verbal comment — a blunt verbal attack delivered with the force of a well-aimed projectile. The term derives from its literal origins as a fragment of brick, evoking the image of something crude and heavy hurled at a target. In modern usage, brickbats are typically directed at specific individuals, organizations, or ideas, and are designed to wound rather than to persuade. Unlike satire or reasoned critique, a brickbat conveys disapproval primarily through its sheer offensiveness and personal sting. The word frequently appears in political discourse, where it sits alongside terms like barbs, potshots, and broadsides as a way to characterize sharp, unsparing censure.
