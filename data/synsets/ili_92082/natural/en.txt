A plier or plyer is a person who regularly engages in and practices a particular trade or craft. The term emphasizes the active, ongoing nature of skilled work — not merely possessing knowledge of a trade, but continuously applying it in practice. Historically, "plyer" referred to someone who plied (worked steadily at) a specific occupation, such as a wheelwright, cooper, or weaver. The word belongs to a family of occupational terms derived from the verb "ply," meaning to work at diligently. In modern usage, "plier" is relatively uncommon compared to more specific terms like artisan, craftsman, or tradesperson, but it retains the connotation of someone committed to the steady exercise of their craft.
