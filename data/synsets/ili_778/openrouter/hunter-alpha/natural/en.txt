The adjective "bulletproof" describes a material, object, or surface that cannot be penetrated by projectiles fired from firearms. This property is achieved through specialized construction — typically involving hardened metals, layered fibers, or ceramic composites that absorb and dissipate the kinetic energy of incoming rounds. Bulletproof protection is most commonly associated with body armor worn by law enforcement and military personnel, as well as reinforced panels installed in vehicles and buildings. The term also extends figuratively to describe arguments, plans, or systems that are impervious to criticism or attack, though the literal sense pertains specifically to resistance against gunfire.
