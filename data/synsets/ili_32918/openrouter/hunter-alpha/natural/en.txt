To yield up is to surrender or relinquish something as a result of external pressure or force, rather than through voluntary choice. This verb describes the act of giving in to compulsion — whether the force is physical, as when a besieged fortress yields up its defenders, or psychological, as when prolonged coercion compels someone to hand over possessions or control. The phrase carries connotations of reluctant compliance: the subject does not choose to give but is compelled to do so. In legal and military contexts, to yield up often implies a formal transfer of authority or territory under duress, distinguishing it from ceding or gifting, which may involve willing parties. The hypernym of this concept is the broader act of relinquishing possession or control, while yielding up adds the specific nuance that the relinquishment results from an inability to resist the applied force.
