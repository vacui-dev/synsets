The Bachelor of Arts (BA) is an undergraduate academic degree awarded upon completion of a program of study in the arts, humanities, social sciences, or natural sciences. As the most widely conferred bachelor's degree worldwide, it represents the foundational credential in the liberal arts tradition, emphasizing broad intellectual development across multiple disciplines rather than narrow vocational training. The BA is typically earned through a four-year curriculum at colleges and universities, and holders of this degree are qualified for entry into graduate study or a wide range of professional careers. The designation "Artium Baccalaureus" (AB) is the Latin equivalent used by some prestigious institutions, and the degree holds equivalent status across most higher education systems globally.
