A teashop, also known as a teahouse or tearoom, is an establishment where tea and light refreshments are served. These venues are central to tea culture across East Asia and the British Isles, functioning as social spaces where visitors gather to enjoy brewed tea alongside snacks such as pastries and sandwiches. Historically, teahouses evolved from roadside rest stops into refined settings for the afternoon tea tradition, a practice deeply embedded in British cuisine. Unlike casual cafés or full-scale restaurants, teashops specialize in a leisurely tea-drinking experience, offering customers a tranquil atmosphere to savor their beverages and light meals.
