Calomel is a tasteless, colorless powder — specifically the chemical compound mercurous chloride (Hg₂Cl₂). It was once widely employed in European medical practice as a cathartic to promote bowel evacuation. The preparation was commonly dispensed in mixture with lime water for external application to syphilitic lesions. Mercury constitutes its primary metallic component, and the substance ultimately fell into disfavor due to the toxic properties of mercury and the availability of safer pharmaceutical alternatives.
