Shooting, also commonly referred to as a shot, is the act of firing a projectile toward a target, typically by means of a weapon such as a firearm, bow, or artillery piece. The term encompasses the entire process from the discharge of the weapon to the projectile's flight, and is used in both military and civilian contexts. In military usage, shooting forms a core component of combat operations, ranging from small-arms fire to coordinated artillery barrages. In sporting contexts, shooting refers to disciplined marksmanship practiced in activities such as target shooting, clay pigeon shooting, and biathlon competitions. The concept is closely related to the broader act of propelling, and includes various specialized forms such as discharging a gun, retaliatory fire, and aimed shots at specific targets.
