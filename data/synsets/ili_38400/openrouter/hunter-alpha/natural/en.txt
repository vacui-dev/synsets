In golf, a downswing is the phase of the golf stroke in which the clubhead moves downward from the top of the backswing toward the ball. It is the critical descending motion that generates clubhead speed and determines the angle of attack at impact. The downswing follows the backswing and precedes the moment of contact with the golf ball. Proper sequencing of the downswing—where the lower body initiates the movement before the upper body and arms—is considered fundamental to an effective golf swing. A poorly timed or off-plane downswing often results in errant shots such as slices, hooks, or fat contacts where the club strikes the ground before the ball.
