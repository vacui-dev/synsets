A highway engineer is a civil engineer who specializes in the planning, design, construction, and maintenance of roads, highways, and related transportation infrastructure. These professionals apply principles of structural engineering, materials science, and traffic analysis to create safe and efficient road networks that connect communities and facilitate commerce. Highway engineers may work for government transportation departments, private consulting firms, or construction companies, overseeing projects that range from rural road improvements to complex urban freeway interchanges. Their work requires expertise in drainage systems, pavement design, earthwork, and environmental impact assessment, as well as knowledge of applicable building codes and safety regulations. The field is closely related to transportation engineering and geotechnical engineering, and highway engineers often collaborate with urban planners, environmental scientists, and public officials to address the evolving needs of modern transportation systems.
