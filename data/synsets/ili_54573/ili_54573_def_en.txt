A hallstand is a freestanding piece of furniture traditionally placed in entryways, hallways, or vestibules of homes, hotels, and public buildings. It serves as a dedicated station for storing outerwear and accessories, featuring hooks or pegs for hanging coats, hats, scarves, and umbrellas, and typically incorporates a mirror for last-minute appearance checks before leaving. Some designs include a shelf or small drawer for gloves, keys, or other small items, and a drip tray or basin underneath for wet umbrellas. Hallstands were particularly popular in Victorian and Edwardian-era homes and remain common in traditional or classic interior designs, though modern variants with minimalist aesthetics are also produced. The furniture piece combines practical storage functionality with decorative elements, often crafted from wood such as oak, mahogany, or walnut, and may feature ornate carvings, brass fittings, or other decorative details that complement the surrounding decor.
