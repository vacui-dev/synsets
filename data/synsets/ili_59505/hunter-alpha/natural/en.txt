A stave is one of several narrow, elongated wooden slats that form the curved sides of a barrel, bucket, or similar cylindrical container. In traditional cooperage, these planks are typically cut from oak or other hardwoods and beveled along their edges so they fit tightly together to create a watertight seal. The staves are arranged radially around the circumference of the vessel and held in place by metal or wooden hoops. The number, width, and thickness of the staves vary depending on the size and intended use of the container. When properly crafted and seasoned, stave-built vessels can last for decades, making them essential components in the storage and aging of wine, whisky, and other liquids.
