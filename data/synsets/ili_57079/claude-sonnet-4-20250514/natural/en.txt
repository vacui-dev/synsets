A peavy (also known as peavey, cant dog, or dog hook) is a stout lever equipped with a sharp spike, primarily used by loggers to handle and maneuver heavy logs. The tool features a pivoting steel hook that bites into the wood, allowing workers to roll, turn, and position logs with mechanical advantage. Invented by Joseph Peavy in the 1850s, the peavy became an essential implement in the lumber industry. A related variant, the cant dog, substitutes a curved hook for the sharp spike but serves the same purpose. Both tools are classified as levers—a type of simple machine—and remain widely used in modern forestry operations.
