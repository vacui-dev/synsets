Nonverbal communication refers to the transmission of meaning and information without the use of spoken or written words. It encompasses a wide range of behaviors, including facial expressions, body posture, gestures, eye contact, and vocal tone — all of which convey emotional states, social intentions, and interpersonal dynamics. In human interaction, nonverbal cues often operate alongside verbal language, enriching or sometimes contradicting the literal content of speech. Research in psychology and linguistics has shown that nonverbal signals can constitute a significant portion of interpersonal communication, sometimes accounting for more impact than words alone. This form of communication is not unique to humans; many animal species rely on nonverbal displays such as body movements, color changes, and scent markings to coordinate social behavior and signal threats or attraction.
