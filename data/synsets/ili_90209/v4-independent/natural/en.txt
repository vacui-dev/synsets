A girlfriend is a female romantic partner in an intimate relationship, typically used to describe a woman or girl who is dating or involved romantically with another person, most commonly a man. The term occupies an intermediate position in the hierarchy of romantic relationship terminology, falling between casual dating and more formally recognized partnerships such as engagement or marriage. Unlike a wife or fiancée, a girlfriend carries no legal or institutional standing, instead serving as a social designation that signals romantic exclusivity and emotional attachment between two people. In contemporary usage, the term is widely applied across diverse relationship structures and has increasingly been adopted in same-sex relationships as well. The word can also carry connotations ranging from lighthearted and youthful to deeply committed, depending on cultural context, age of the individuals, and duration of the relationship.
