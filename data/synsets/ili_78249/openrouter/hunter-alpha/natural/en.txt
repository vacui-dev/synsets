Curry powder is a pungent spice blend originating from the Indian subcontinent, composed primarily of ground cumin, coriander, turmeric, and various additional aromatic spices such as fenugreek, black pepper, and chili. The exact composition varies widely by region and manufacturer, with no single standardized recipe defining the mixture. It is widely used as a seasoning in Indian, Southeast Asian, and global cuisines to impart a warm, earthy flavor and golden-yellow color to curries, stews, soups, marinades, and rice dishes. The blend serves as a convenient condiment that allows cooks to achieve a complex, layered spiciness without measuring individual spices separately.
