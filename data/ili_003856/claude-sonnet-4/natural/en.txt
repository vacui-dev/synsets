Sound sleep refers to a state of deep and complete unconsciousness during rest, characterized by the absence of disturbance or interruption. A person experiencing sound sleep is difficult to awaken and shows minimal movement or response to external stimuli. This type of sleep is considered the most restorative phase of the sleep cycle, essential for physical recovery, memory consolidation, and overall cognitive function. Medical professionals often use terms like profound, heavy, or wakeless to describe this condition when assessing sleep quality in patients.
