A nunnery is a building or complex of buildings where a community of nuns lives together in a religious order. These establishments serve as both residences and places of worship for women who have dedicated their lives to religious service, typically following vows of poverty, chastity, and obedience. Unlike monasteries, which house male religious communities, nunneries are specifically designated for female monastics. Historically, these institutions have provided education, healthcare, and charitable services alongside their primary spiritual functions.
