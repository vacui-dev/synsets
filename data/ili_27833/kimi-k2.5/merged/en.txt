<|ILI_27833|>goad or prick is to poke or prod someone or something with a pointed object, typically a stick, as a means of urging them forward or stimulating action. This <|ILI_007189|>verb describes a physical act of piercing or jabbing that serves as a driving force, often used metaphorically to describe provoking someone into doing something through persistent irritation or incentive. The action implies a sharp, pointed stimulus that compels movement or response, much like how a herdsman might use a pointed stick to encourage cattle to move.
