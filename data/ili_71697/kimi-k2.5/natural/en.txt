Bachelor of Science in Engineering

An undergraduate academic degree awarded to students who have completed a program of study in engineering at a college or university. This degree typically requires four to five years of full-time study and combines foundational mathematics, physics, and chemistry with specialized coursework in fields such as civil, mechanical, electrical, chemical, or computer engineering. Graduates with this qualification are prepared to pursue professional engineering careers, continue to graduate studies, or obtain professional engineering licensure. The degree represents formal recognition of technical competence and foundational knowledge in applying scientific principles to design, build, and maintain structures, machines, systems, and processes.
